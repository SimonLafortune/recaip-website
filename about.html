About Recaip

Automated release communication for teams that ship fast.

Why Recaip exists

Most software teams ship code every day. But the people who use that software rarely find out what changed. Changelogs get skipped. Release notes get written weeks late, if at all. Support teams discover breaking changes from customer tickets.

This is not a tooling problem. It is an ownership problem. Writing release notes falls between roles. Developers focus on code. Product managers focus on roadmaps. Marketing focuses on campaigns. Nobody owns the gap between "code merged" and "user informed."

Recaip fills that gap. It connects to your dev tools, reads what you shipped, and writes the recap automatically. Every PR becomes a changelog entry, a social post, an email draft, and a stakeholder update. No manual work required.

Built by a product leader who got tired of writing changelogs

Recaip is built by Simon Lafortune, a product director based in Quebec, Canada. After years of managing product teams at SaaS companies, Simon noticed the same pattern: teams ship great work, but nobody communicates it to users.

The idea started as a side project in early 2025. Simon built the first version using Claude Code, shipping the MVP in weeks. Recaip is now dogfooding with its own repository, generating its own changelog from its own PRs.

Recaip is an independent, bootstrapped product. No VC funding. No team of 50. Just one builder solving a real problem.

What we believe

Ship, then communicate

Shipping code is only half the job. If users do not know about it, the work is wasted. Recaip makes communication automatic so you can focus on building.

Approve first, automate later

Trust is earned. Every channel starts in manual review mode. You flip to autopilot only when you are ready. No surprises.

Simple, stackable pricing

One price, unlimited products, no per-seat charges. Need more capacity? Stack packs. No surprises.

AI as a teammate, not a toy

Recaip is not a chatbot you prompt. It is an autonomous agent that runs in the background, reads your code, and does its job without being asked.
